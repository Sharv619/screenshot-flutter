# Use-Case Journey: Screenshot Intelligent Actions

## Overview
This document outlines the user journey for specific categories detected by the Screenshot Auto-Categorizer: **Locations** and **Books**.

---

## Use Case 1: Location & Restaurant Discovery
**Trigger:** User takes a screenshot of a restaurant on Instagram, a location on a blog, or a map snippet.

### Journey:
1.  **Detection:** The app detects a new screenshot.
2.  **Analysis:** OCR and entity recognition identify address patterns, restaurant names, or geographic coordinates.
3.  **Categorization:** The screenshot is assigned to the **'Locations'** folder.
4.  **Interaction (The Pop-up):**
    *   A bottom sheet or pop-up notification appears when the user opens the screenshot in the app.
    *   **Map Preview:** A small interactive or static Google Maps snippet showing the exact location.
5.  **User Options:**
    *   **Save to List:** Adds the location to a 'Must-Visit' or 'Travel' list within the app for future reference.
    *   **Open with GMaps:** Launches the Google Maps app with the destination pre-filled for immediate navigation.
6.  **Outcome:** The user successfully captures a place of interest and has an immediate path to action (navigation or bookmarking).

---

## Use Case 2: Book Research & Acquisition
**Trigger:** User takes a screenshot of a book cover, a reading list, or an Amazon product page.

### Journey:
1.  **Detection:** The app detects a new screenshot.
2.  **Analysis:** OCR extracts the book title and author. Image classification recognizes the book cover structure.
3.  **Categorization:** The screenshot is assigned to the **'Education/Reading'** or a new **'Books'** folder.
4.  **Interaction:**
    *   The app displays a detail overlay on the screenshot.
    *   **Metadata:** Title, Author, and a brief description fetched via API (e.g., Google Books).
5.  **User Options:**
    *   **Amazon Link:** A direct button to the Amazon product page for purchasing the physical or Kindle version.
    *   **Audible Link:** A direct button to the Audible store for the audiobook version.
    *   **Save to List:** Adds to an internal 'Reading List' or 'Wishlist'.
6.  **Outcome:** The user seamlessly transitions from "capturing a recommendation" to "acquiring the book" in their preferred format.
